Sales page prompt
Claude — full sales page

Write a sales page for [product name] at $[price]. Audience: [describe]. Tone: direct, no fluff, confident — not hypey. Structure: hook (1 sentence problem) → agitate (3 bullet pain points) → solution (what this is) → what's inside (3-5 bullet points) → who this is for → price + what they get → CTA. Max 300 words. ChatGPT — handle top 5 objections

My product is [product name] at $[price]. The 5 most common objections are: 1) [objection] 2) [objection] 3) [objection] 4) [objection] 5) [objection]. Write a confident, direct 2-sentence response to each. No begging. No discounting. Reframe the value each time.

Area 2 — Products

Build products people want.

Most people build first and validate later. This is backwards. Use AI to validate before you spend a single hour creating.

The validation process
1

Validate the idea first

Before you build anything — run the validation prompt. Find out if people are already searching for this, what they're willing to pay and who the competition is.

2

Name the product

Run the naming prompt. Get 10 name options. Pick the one that communicates the outcome clearly and sounds like a brand.

3

Build the outline with AI

Give AI your product concept. Ask for a complete outline — sections, subsections, what to cover in each. This is your blueprint. You fill in the expertise.

4

Write section by section

Take the outline section by section into ChatGPT or Claude. You provide the knowledge — AI structures and writes it. Edit for your voice. Done.

Product prompts
Perplexity — validate a product idea

I want to create a digital product about [topic] for [audience]. Research: 1) Is there demand for this? What are people searching for? 2) What products already exist and at what price points? 3) What do people complain is missing from existing products? 4) What would make a new product stand out? Give me sourced data where possible.

ChatGPT — 10 product name ideas

Generate 10 product name ideas for a digital product that helps [audience] achieve [outcome]. Names should: be short (2-4 words), communicate the result clearly, sound like a brand not a course. Label each with a one-sentence explanation of why it works. No names with "ultimate," "complete," or "master."

Claude — full product outline

Create a complete outline for a digital product called "[product name]" for [audience] at $[price]. The product should help them [specific outcome]. Structure: introduction + [number] main sections + conclusion. For each section: title, 3-4 subsections with one-line description of what's covered. The outline should feel like a clear system, not a course.

Area 3 — Customers

Never write the same reply twice.

Save every good reply you write as a prompt template. Within 30 days you have a complete customer service system that runs itself.

Build your reply library

FAQ responses

Write the definitive answer to each FAQ once with AI. Save it. Copy-paste forever. Or add it to your Stan.store page to prevent the question entirely.

Complaint handling

Acknowledge → apologize if warranted → resolve → close. AI writes this without emotion. You stay professional even when the customer isn't.

Refund requests

Two versions: approve with grace, or decline with firm reasoning. Both written professionally. Neither burns the relationship.

Follow-up sequences

3-email follow-up sequence for customers who bought but haven't engaged. Reminds them the product exists. Drives usage and reduces refund risk.

Upsell after purchase

24-hour post-purchase message that introduces your second product naturally. Warm, not pushy. Written in your voice.

Testimonial request

Ask for a review at the right moment — after they've had a win. AI writes the message. You send it. Most people say yes when asked well.

Customer service prompts
ChatGPT — handle a complaint

A customer sent this complaint: "[paste complaint]". Write a professional, empathetic response that: acknowledges their frustration, takes responsibility where appropriate, offers a clear resolution, and closes the conversation positively. Tone: calm, direct, solution-focused. Max 100 words. Do not grovel.

ChatGPT — request a testimonial

Write a short message asking a customer for a testimonial. They bought [product name] [timeframe] ago. Tone: warm, personal, direct — not corporate. Ask them specifically: what they were struggling with before, what changed after using the product, and who they'd recommend it to. Max 80 words. Make it easy to say yes.

Claude — 3-email follow-up sequence

Write a 3-email follow-up sequence for customers who bought [product name] but haven't engaged yet. Email 1 (day 3): check in, remind them of the value. Email 2 (day 7): share one quick win they can get today. Email 3 (day 14): direct — ask if they need help getting started. Each email: max 100 words. Subject line included. Tone: Alexandra — direct, caring, no fluff.

Area 4 — Research

Know your market before you move.

Most people skip research and wonder why nothing sells. AI makes research fast enough that there's no excuse to skip it.

What to research and when

Before launching a product

Validate demand. Check competition. Understand what's already out there and what gap you fill. Use Perplexity — takes 10 minutes, saves you weeks of wasted work.

Before setting a price

Search what competitors charge. Find the market range. Position above or below based on your differentiation — not based on what feels safe.

Before creating content

Find out what your audience is actually asking. Perplexity + Reddit + TikTok comments. Real language from real people = better hooks and captions.

Monthly trend check

15 minutes per month. What's growing in your niche? What are people talking about? Stay ahead of the content wave instead of chasing it.

Research prompts
Perplexity — competitor analysis

Analyse the competitive landscape for [your niche/product type]. Find: 1) The top 5 competitors and their price points, 2) What customers say they love about existing products (look at reviews), 3) What customers say is missing or frustrating, 4) Any gaps in the market that aren't being served. Give me a summary I can act on.

Perplexity — niche validation

I'm considering building an online business in [niche]. Research: 1) Is there proven demand — what are people searching for and buying? 2) What's the typical price range for products in this niche? 3) Who are the biggest players and what makes them successful? 4) What would a newcomer need to do differently to stand out in 2025/2026?

ChatGPT — find content angles from real pain

Here are 10 real comments/questions from my audience: [paste comments]. Analyse them and: 1) Identify the 3 deepest underlying frustrations, 2) Suggest 5 content angles that directly address these frustrations, 3) Write one hook for each angle. Use the exact language from the comments where possible.

Area 6 — Marketing

A week of content in 45 minutes.

Hooks, captions, Reel scripts, carousels. AI writes the drafts. You edit for your voice. Post. Repeat.

The weekly content system
1

Sunday — pick 7 topics (10 min)

2× pain posts, 2× value posts, 1× story, 1× result/proof, 1× selling post with trigger word.

2

Run the batch prompt (5 min)

One ChatGPT prompt → 7 complete captions. Copy. Done. See Prompt Bank for the exact prompt.

3

Edit for your voice (30 min)

5 minutes per caption. Read out loud — fix anything that doesn't sound like you. Don't over-edit.

4

Schedule in Meta Business Suite (15 min)

business.facebook.com → schedule all 7 posts for the week. Best times: 7-9am, 12-2pm, 6-9pm.
